Engineering Principles & Practices
  • Resilient system design: Architect systems to handle partial failures gracefully, ensuring service continuity during downstream outages and degraded conditions
  • Scalability & performance alignment: Design for real-world workload patterns with dynamic scaling, avoiding over-provisioning based on theoretical peak assumptions
  • Pragmatic architecture evolution: Emphasize incremental, maintainable enhancements over premature optimization or unnecessary complexity
  • Infrastructure as Code (IaC): Ensure environment consistency, repeatability, and version-controlled infrastructure across all stages
  • CI/CD-driven delivery: Automate infrastructure and application deployments to eliminate manual errors, reduce drift, and improve release velocity
  • Security by design: Enforce least-privilege access, eliminate static credentials through IAM-based authentication, maintain full auditability of changes, and apply guardrails for governed AI adoption
